BMW Aftersales 2026 Available Non-MACO Co-Op Claim Guide

Executive-clean working guide for the four available non-MACO co-op categories dealers are most likely to use: promotional events & sponsorships, approved apparel, showroom accessorized vehicles, and charitable donations.

What this page helps with

  • Quickly identify what qualifies for 50% reimbursement from the Marketing fund.
  • Spot the documentation required before a claim is submitted.
  • Avoid the most common denial triggers called out in program rules.

Best use case

  • Internal planning before spend is committed.
  • Fast claim readiness check before using the BMW Co-op Program Web Tool.

At a glance

Which categories are available?

This guide covers only the four categories requested and only the grounded facts provided from the BMW Aftersales Dealer Co-op Programs reference.

4 claim paths

1) Promotional events & sponsorships

  • 50% reimbursement from Marketing fund.
  • Must be pre-approved via the BMW Aftersales Event Co-op Pre-Approval Form.
  • Form must be signed by an Aftersales Area Manager, Area Manager, Regional Aftersales Development Manager, or Regional Aftersales Manager.

2) Approved apparel

  • 50% reimbursement from Marketing fund.
  • Must be an Original BMW Lifestyle item found on shop.bmwusa.com.
  • Requires a parts invoice with BMW part number, item name/description, and quantity.

3) Showroom accessorized vehicles

  • 50% reimbursement from Marketing fund.
  • Must use an Original BMW Accessory found on shop.bmwusa.com.
  • Requires a $1,000 minimum accessory MSRP total.

4) Charitable donations

  • 50% reimbursement from Marketing fund.
  • Charity must be a registered 501(c)(3).
  • Donation must be an Original BMW Part, Accessory, or Lifestyle item.

Events & sponsorships

The highest-risk category deserves its own checklist

Promotional events have the tightest process controls and the clearest auto-denial trigger.

30-day rule

Deadline and approval chain

  1. Submit the BMW Aftersales Event Co-op Pre-Approval Form.
  2. Secure signature from an Aftersales Area Manager, Area Manager, Regional Aftersales Development Manager, or Regional Aftersales Manager.
  3. Ensure the submission is approved at least 30 days before the event.
  4. If there is no approved pre-approval 30 days prior, the claim is automatically denied.

Event caps by UIO

  • Under 11,999 UIO: $3,000 reimbursement cap.
  • 12,000–24,999 UIO: $4,000 reimbursement cap.
  • Over 25,000 UIO: $7,000 reimbursement cap.
Best bets

Events with clean pre-approval timing, itemized original receipts, and eligible giveaway items limited to Original BMW Parts, Accessories, and Lifestyle items are the most operationally straightforward.

Hardest to get approved

Anything submitted inside the 30-day window, or events where the final spend drifts more than $750 from the approved total, carries obvious risk.

Do not submit

New Owner Clinics and Monthly/Quarterly Association meetings do not qualify.

For promotional events and sponsorships, all claims go through the BMW Co-op Program Web Tool, and the claim deadline is within 45 days of the event/ad date or invoice date, whichever is shorter.

Practical guidance

Fast read: what tends to be easiest vs. what needs the most discipline

These are operational observations based strictly on the stated rule complexity and required proof.

Typically easiest to document

  • Approved apparel: narrow product definition, known source, and one invoice package.
  • Charitable donations: manageable if 501(c)(3) proof and donated item invoice are assembled together.

Usually hardest to keep clean

  • Promotional events & sponsorships: pre-approval timing, signature routing, itemization, receipt retention, reimbursement caps, and variance control all matter.
  • Showroom accessorized vehicles: easy to lose momentum if the photo, invoice details, wholesale pricing backup, or $1,000 MSRP threshold are incomplete.

Quick submission checklist

  • Confirm the category matches one of the four approved paths on this page.
  • Verify the item is an Original BMW Part, Accessory, or Lifestyle item when required.
  • Collect the exact proof listed for that category before submission.
  • Check timing rules before the spend ages out.

Event-specific exclusion watch

For promotional events, production, loading, startup, handling charges, agency commissions, taxes, and shipping are not reimbursed.

Operational note: if an event plan is not formally approved 30 days before the event, treat it as non-claimable under this category.
