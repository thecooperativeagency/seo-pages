If you're cross-shopping the 2026 BMW X5 and the 2026 Lexus GX, you're looking at two strong luxury SUVs — but they approach the segment very differently. The GX leans truck-based and rugged. The X5 leans performance-forward and refined. Here's exactly how they stack up on the numbers that matter.

The Bottom Line

For most luxury SUV buyers in 2026, the BMW X5 is the stronger all-around choice.

  • Faster — 5.1 seconds to 60 mph vs 6.5 seconds in the GX
  • More fuel efficient — up to 6 MPG better city, saving $400–$600/year
  • More cargo space — 72.3 cu ft folded vs 49.3 cu ft in the GX
  • Lower starting price — $67,300 vs $67,735
  • More included maintenance — 3 years vs 2 service visits

The Lexus GX wins on towing capacity (9,063 lbs) and comes standard with 3-row seating — making it a better choice for buyers who regularly haul heavy loads or need the extra row. For daily driving, performance, and efficiency, the X5 wins on nearly every metric.
