The Two Most Cross-Shopped Compact Luxury SAVs

The GLC and X3 are the two most cross-shopped luxury compact SAVs in America. Both are excellent. Both are built in Germany. Both will get you where you're going in comfort and style. But they're built with fundamentally different priorities — and once you understand that difference, the choice becomes clear. Here's how they actually stack up.

Performance: Nearly Identical on Paper. Not in Practice.

The numbers look close. The experience doesn't feel that way.

Spec BMW X3 xDrive30 Mercedes GLC 300
Horsepower 255 hp 258 hp
Torque 295 lb-ft 295 lb-ft
0–60 mph 5.6 sec ✓ 5.9 sec
AWD System xDrive (sport-tuned, rear-biased) ✓ 4MATIC (comfort-tuned)
Steering Feel Driver-focused ✓ Comfort-focused

Nearly identical on paper. The X3 is 0.3 seconds faster and far more engaging to actually drive. BMW's xDrive is rear-biased — it feels like a BMW should. Mercedes' 4MATIC is comfort-focused — it feels like a luxury crossover. Neither is wrong. But one of them is a driver's machine.

Advantage: BMW X3
