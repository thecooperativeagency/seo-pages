More Than an SUV

The X5 invented a category. When BMW launched it in 1999, nobody believed a luxury vehicle could be this capable off-road and this rewarding on it. 25 years later, the formula is still unmatched. The 2026 X5 is bigger, more powerful, and more technologically advanced than ever — yet it still drives like a BMW. That's the trick no competitor has figured out.

The xDrive40i starts with a 3.0L TwinPower Turbo inline-six producing 375 horsepower. Step up to the M60i and you get BMW's legendary 4.4L V8 — 523 horsepower, 0-60 in 3.9 seconds. Every X5 comes standard with BMW's xDrive all-wheel drive, now with optional adaptive air suspension that reads the road and adjusts in real time. Need to haul? The X5 tows up to 7,200 lbs. Need to seat the whole family? The optional third row fits up to 7 passengers without sacrificing the rear-seat luxury the X5 is known for. This is what it looks like when performance and practicality aren't compromises — they're the whole point.

Built for Louisiana Life

Louisiana doesn't go easy on vehicles. Summer heat that warps lesser machines, coastal storms, long interstate hauls between Baton Rouge and New Orleans, boat trailers on the weekends, and families that need space without apology. The X5 was built for exactly this.

BMW's xDrive all-wheel drive keeps you planted whether you're pushing through a sudden downpour on I-10 or navigating a flooded access road. The optional adaptive air suspension reads Louisiana's notoriously uneven roads and adjusts continuously — potholes become non-events. The standard panoramic sunroof fills the cabin with light on clear days. When the family needs room, the available third row delivers seven-passenger seating without the penalty of a compromised interior. This is the vehicle Baton Rouge was built for.
