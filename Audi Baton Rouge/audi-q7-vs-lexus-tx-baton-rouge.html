The Lexus TX and Audi Q7 both target luxury 3-row buyers. Both are polished, refined, and premium. But when you compare real-world performance, standard features, and what you actually get for your money, the Q7 wins on every category that matters to Louisiana families.

Which Is More Powerful?

Metric Audi Q7 Lexus TX 350
Horsepower 335 hp 275 hp
Engine 3.0L turbocharged V6 2.4L turbo 4-cylinder
0–60 mph 5.7 sec ~7.2 sec

The Q7 delivers 60 more horsepower from a true V6 engine versus the TX's four-cylinder. That gap is felt every time you merge on I-10 or accelerate to highway speed with a full passenger load.

▲ Winner: Audi Q7

Louisiana gets rain, flooding, and unpredictable roads. The Q7's standard quattro AWD gives you confidence in every condition without paying a premium. With the Lexus TX, AWD costs extra — and the base version leaves you with front-wheel drive in a premium SUV.

▲ Winner: Audi Q7

Which Has More Seating?

Audi Q7
7 seats standard. All three rows are genuinely usable — not just for small children.
Lexus TX
3-row body, but the third row is cramped — best suited for children, not adults.

For Baton Rouge families — school runs, road trips, hauling the whole crew — the Q7's standard 7-seat layout with genuinely usable third-row space is a real practical advantage over the TX's cramped rear bench.

▲ Winner: Audi Q7

Which Interior Is Better?

Audi Q7
Audi Virtual Cockpit digital instrument cluster, MMI infotainment, clean European design, premium materials throughout.
Lexus TX
Lexus touchscreen and analog gauges. Quality materials, but a busier, less intuitive layout than the Q7's cockpit.

The Q7's Virtual Cockpit puts a fully digital, customizable instrument display in front of the driver — a level of tech and clarity the TX doesn't match at base trim. The Q7 interior feels driver-focused and intuitive; the TX is nice but more conventional.

▲ Winner: Audi Q7
