The Lexus TX is Lexus's newest 3-row entry, and it's a capable vehicle. But when you compare it head-to-head with the Audi Q7, the performance gap is hard to ignore.

Performance: Advantage Q7

Metric Audi Q7 Lexus TX 350
Horsepower 335 hp 275 hp
Torque 369 lb-ft 317 lb-ft
0–60 mph 5.5 sec 7.8 sec
AWD Standard quattro Optional (extra cost)

The Q7 reaches 60 mph 2.3 seconds faster. That's not a small difference — it's a completely different driving experience. And while the TX charges extra for AWD, every Q7 comes with legendary quattro standard.

▲ Advantage: Audi Q7

Where the TX Has an Edge

The TX does have more cargo space behind the third row, and can seat up to 8 passengers. If maximum cargo capacity is your top priority, the TX is worth a look. But if you care about how the SUV actually drives — how it feels on the highway, how fast it responds when you need it — the Q7 wins by a significant margin.

Value: Advantage Q7

When you add AWD to the TX to match the Q7's standard capability, the prices converge — but the performance gap doesn't. The Q7 gives you more power, faster acceleration, and standard AWD at a competitive price. You're paying the same money for a significantly better-driving vehicle.

▲ Advantage: Audi Q7

Built for Louisiana Driving

Louisiana weather doesn't give you a warning. Summer storms roll in fast on I-10, and the roads between Baton Rouge and New Orleans demand a vehicle you can trust in the wet. The Q7's standard quattro AWD means you're always ready — no extra cost, no option box to check. And on long highway drives where you want to feel the engine respond, the Q7's 335 horsepower delivers where the TX can't keep up.
